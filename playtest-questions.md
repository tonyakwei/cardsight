# Playtest Questions

Questions to ask (or at least think about) after the first playtest of Twisting Tales.

## Core Questions

- How was the process of acquiring cards? Was it collaborative? Competitive?
- What was the process of cataloging your items to achieve your missions? Did it seem too long? Was it kind of fun seeing what tools you had at your disposal?
- How was the process of reading your story card and seeing the missions? Was the text too long? How did you decide what to do, with limited time?
- How did it feel receiving your mission consequences?
- Did it feel like the story was slowly making sense as time went on?

## Additional Considerations

**Trading & social dynamics**
- How did trading between houses work in practice? Did people negotiate, or just swap blindly?
- Did any house hoard cards? Did that feel strategic or frustrating?
- How much cross-house conversation happened organically?

**The phone/scanning experience**
- Did scanning feel quick and seamless, or was there friction (camera issues, loading)?
- Did the physical card flash feel like a moment, or did people not notice it?
- Did the splash gate (EXAMINE button) create the intended tension, or was it just a speed bump?
- How long did people actually look at their phone screen before putting it down?

**Puzzle solving & physical materials**
- Did people actually use the printed materials (maps, star charts) to solve, or default to phone?
- Was the complexity difference between simple and complex cards clear?
- Did anyone get stuck on an answer and give up?

**Pacing & time pressure**
- Did each act feel like the right length?
- Was there a lull or dead zone mid-act?
- Did the act break (card collection/redistribution) feel smooth or disruptive?

**Showtime**
- Did the synchronized press feel dramatic? Did people count down together?
- Was the reveal satisfying?

**Host experience**
- How much did the host need to intervene vs. let the game run?
- Was the console useful in the moment, or too much to track?

**Overall**
- What was the single highest-energy moment of the night?
- What moment felt most confusing?
- Would you play again? Would you bring different friends?
