# Act 3 History Timeline Notes

## Purpose

Act 3 opens with a short shared history reconstruction before the negotiation phase.

This phase exists to:

- get all houses onto the same factual baseline
- show that the Source was genuinely valuable before it became catastrophic
- provide balanced argumentative ammunition for Jones, Croft, and Drake

## Era Structure

Use three eras:

1. `Discovery`
2. `Dependency`
3. `Consent`

The timeline should be **braided**, not ideological. Each era should contain:

- one Jones-leaning card
- one Croft-leaning card
- one Drake-leaning card
- one hinge card

## Historical Dimensions

The reconstructed history should support all three house instincts:

### Jones

The Source was a real gift.

- It accelerated learning.
- It improved mathematics, language, and scholarship.
- It raised ordinary people into extraordinary thinkers.

### Croft

The Source corrupted judgment.

- It worked like a danger people could not safely interpret on their own.
- It bent priorities, appetite, and restraint.
- The QRians' original warnings were too obscure to protect later people.

### Drake

The QRians eventually accepted hard measures.

- Self-regulation failed.
- Routes were closed because people could no longer be trusted with access.
- The final temple was intentional prison architecture, not passive worship space.

## Writing Rules For History Cards

History cards should be easy to imagine and easy to read on first pass.

- Use simple language.
- Give each card one human moment, one object, or one clear incident.
- Make chronology easier by embedding a light temporal cue under the title.
- Early cards should show the value of the Source very clearly.
- Early Croft/Drake cards should feel precautionary, not already condemning.
- Tragedy should emerge through contrast, not purple prose.

Good title style:

- `Letter from a Schoolmaster`
- `Clinic Record: First Exposure`
- `Order Limiting Second Visits`

Avoid vague or over-poetic titles.

This sample best captures the intended tone and clarity:

>

>
