# Croft Company — Act 1 Story Card

**CROFT COMPANY**

You are the Croft Company — handpicked by the legendary Lara Croft herself. Friends, hired hands, and devoted followers of the most relentless relic hunter alive. When Croft caught wind of this temple, she dispatched you immediately, convinced — in that way only she can be — that this would be the next biggest thing. You trust her. You're also a little terrified of her.

Lara isn't here. You are. Make her proud.

You dug your way in from below, tomb-raider style, and emerged to find company. Near a freshly blasted hole in the wall, a crew in tactical gear and balaclavas is scanning the perimeter — the Drake Delegation. They used dynamite. Across the chamber, a group clutching whips and pencils is already hunched over inscriptions — the Jones Junket. You look much more stylish. The water is rising — probably thanks to whatever that blast hole did to the foundation.

Tie your hair back. Let's go.

---

**MISSIONS**

**1. Recover the Grappling Rigs**
Your signature grappling rigs — high-tension cable, Croft-issue hooks, the works — are stashed on the ledge where you climbed in, eight feet up. The floodwater is rising toward them. There's a dry alcove higher up the wall, well above the waterline — if you can rig a haul route, the gear is safe. Your route-setting kit spilled across the chamber floor in the first surge — bolts, clamps, pulleys, all unmarked steel hardware with no labels or manufacturer stamps. Conspicuously anonymous equipment. You've spotted some pieces, but the other teams have grabbed the rest of the Unmarked Steel Hardware. You could acquire them back...

**2. Solve the Stone Jigsaw**
Water pours through a crack in the far wall where a sealed passage gave way. Scattered across the floor — odd blocks with symbols marked along their edges and a letter on each face. They're clearly meant to fit together somehow. The other teams have grabbed some of the Odd Edge-Marked Blocks. This is what you were built for...

**3. Reach the Impossible Vase**
Far off to the left, right at the fuzzy edge of the shadows — atop a ledge fifteen feet above the floor, a vibrantly red, sparkling porcelain vase. It catches the torchlight in flashes — impossibly intact and clean after all these years. Nothing survives centuries in a sealed chamber looking like it was fired yesterday. Metal spokes with inscriptions etched into them are scattered around the chamber — potential footholds. You've spotted some nearby, but the other teams have picked up some of the Inscribed Metal Spokes. Fifteen feet is nothing for this crew. You could get up there...

**4. Activate the Teaching Stone**
In the dead center of the chamber — a wide stone disc on a pedestal, pale and luminous. Two concentric rings of number slots scored into its face — some filled, some empty. Small bone tokens with numbers carved into them lie scattered near the base — palm-sized, smooth, clearly meant to fit into the disc's slots. The other teams have grabbed some of the Numbered Bone Tokens. Lara would already be elbow-deep in this thing. You could try negotiating for the missing pieces...

**5. Follow the Drag Marks**
Against the near wall, low and easy to miss — a squat doorway set into stone rougher than the rest, as if finished in a hurry. Beyond it, darkness. On the floor, deep parallel grooves gouged into stone, wide as a body, dragging inward. Your team isn't afraid of dark corridors. Ancient pottery shards with strange etchings lie scattered near the entrance, though the other teams have already picked up some of the Ancient Pottery Shards. You could follow the marks and see what's at the end...
