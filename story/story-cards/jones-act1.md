# Jones Junket — Act 1 Story Card

**JONES JUNKET**

You are the Jones Junket — scholars, disciples, and lifelong devotees of the legendary Indiana Jones. You have spent years at his seminars, absorbing every word, preparing for this very moment: your maiden expedition. Three months of painstaking schematic analysis revealed a secret entrance into the temple's lower level. Three months! And worth every second.

If you succeed here, the great Indiana Jones will finally retire, and your careers begin. No pressure.

You entered through the hidden passage to find you are not alone. Across the chamber, a crew in tactical gear and balaclavas is inspecting the walls near a blast hole — the Drake Delegation, apparently. They used *dynamite*. Near the far wall, a team in jorts and styled hair is pulling at the stone — the Croft Company. Neither group appears to have brought pencils. The water is rising — and given the blast hole in the wall, it's not hard to guess why.

Your notes didn't mention flooding. Adapt.

---

**MISSIONS**

**1. Retrieve the Ceremonial Whips**
In the rush through the hidden passage, the ceremonial whips were left behind — and the floodwater is swallowing the way back. The passage branches into several half-submerged corridors, each with a QRian word carved above its entrance. A longer inscription on the nearby wall appears to describe the correct route — Dr. Jones always said the QRians labeled everything. One swimmer, one breath, one chance before the water seals them all. The tablet was clay — like the cuneiform slabs Dr. Jones showed you in his sixth lecture — and it shattered when the floodwater hit. Each fragment is a Coded Clay Tablet bearing a pairing of QRian glyphs to letters. You've gathered a few, but the other teams have pocketed some of the Coded Clay Tablets too...

**2. Activate the Ancient Drainage**
Along the lower walls, half-obscured by mineral deposits — a 3×3 grid of carved pipe sections, clearly an engineered drainage network. The source and drain are visible but seven connecting sections are missing — ceramic tiles with grooves carved into them, scattered across the floor. You didn't spend three months on schematics for nothing. The other teams have been pocketing the Grooved Ceramic Tiles. You could try negotiating...

**3. Sort the Scattered Offerings**
High on the far wall — three deep alcoves, each stained a different color from centuries of offerings. Six stone vessels are scattered on the floor below, knocked loose by the flood. Each vessel still holds traces of whatever was burned or poured inside it, and each has a letter carved into its underside. If you can determine which vessels belong in which alcove, the letters should reveal something about this place. The other teams have picked up some of the Sealed Stone Vessels as curiosities — whatever is sealed inside them clearly piqued their interest. You might try negotiating for them...

**4. Examine the Sliding Panels**
Peering straight across the haze of the chamber — small flat rocks, so precisely placed and stacked on one another that they form a grid in the wall. They can clearly be slid around. But several are missing — smooth Flat Inscribed Slates with QRian symbols etched into them, dislodged and scattered across the chamber floor. The other teams have already picked some of the Flat Inscribed Slates up. At the grid's center: a dark, hand-shaped hollow, worn silk-smooth by what must have been hundreds of palms pressed into it over centuries. This is exactly the kind of mechanism Dr. Jones described in his 42nd lecture. If you could recover the missing panels, you could try the activation sequence...

**5. Map the False Exit**
Something was odd when you first entered the chamber, and now you realize what. Off to the left, beyond a low archway — a corridor that tilts *up*. After a temple where everything descends, an upward slope feels like sunlight. But three months of schematics trained you to map before you move. Waymarkers once lined the corridor walls — Worn Stone Markers, visibly handled over centuries, their edges rounded by countless passing hands. Most have fallen and scattered. The other teams have picked up a few Worn Stone Markers you might try negotiating for...
