# Mission Design

Work-in-progress mission designs. Missions are **house-exclusive** — each house gets its own set, though puzzle types are reused across houses with different answers and narrative framing.

**Important context:**
- The houses did NOT expect to find each other here — each hoped to be the first to the temple
- Mission cards may mention the other houses' names and where they are situated in the temple (flavor text)
- The emotional setting between houses: reluctant cooperation between rivals / competing teams forced together by circumstance
- **The patrons (Lara Croft, Indiana Jones) are NOT present in the temple.** Only representatives of each house are there. The patrons are off in their separate locations.

---

## Act 1 Missions

### Pattern: Crew Missions as Character-Defining Dilemmas

Each house's Crew mission is a **dilemma about a signature tool or item** that the rising floodwater threatens to submerge. The house must decide whether to commit resources (time, scans, trades) to rescue it — or let it go and focus elsewhere. The item is tied to the house's identity and may matter again in later acts.

This pattern reinforces house identity, creates meaningful tradeoffs, and builds character investment in a way that costs nothing narratively if the item is lost (the act moves on).

### Drake Delegation — Act 1

#### Crew Mission: Secure the Fuse Charges

Drake's precision explosives need 7 components to assemble: Whisper Pins, Dragon Teeth, Ember Dust, Striker Plates, Coil Segments, Ghost Shells, Fang Clips. The blast scattered them across the chamber. Other teams have picked some up — the codenames mean nothing to them.

**Puzzle type:** G — Sequence Ordering

**Required clue cards:** 3 cards across different sets (2+3+2 distribution).

| Card | Components |
|------|-----------|
| Card 1 | Whisper Pins & Dragon Teeth |
| Card 2 | Ember Dust, Striker Plates & Coil Segments |
| Card 3 | Ghost Shells & Fang Clips |

Each component description hints at assembly position:
1. **Whisper Pins** — *"Delicate. These seat into the base before anything else."*
2. **Ghost Shells** — *"The outer housing. Nothing goes on after these."*
3. **Dragon Teeth** — *"The heavy cores. They nest right on top of the pins."*
4. **Ember Dust** — *"Volatile. Packed between the cores and the plates. Don't sneeze."*
5. **Striker Plates** — *"Press flat against the dust layer. Takes the initial impact."*
6. **Fang Clips** — *"Lock the plates in place. Snaps onto the strikers before you seal."*
7. **Coil Segments** — *"The ignition thread. Winds through the clips and connects to the shell trigger."*

**Answer:** `1345672` (Whisper Pins → Dragon Teeth → Ember Dust → Striker Plates → Fang Clips → Coil Segments → Ghost Shells)

**Correct answer reveal:**
"Click. Click. Click. Seven components, locked in sequence, just like training. The precision explosives are live. Whether you'll need them down here is another question — but up ahead, in whatever comes next, you'll have options the other teams don't. You just hope they never find out what those codenames actually meant."

**Success consequence:**
"The charges are assembled and secured. The team moves with a little more swagger — you're the only crew in this temple with a real ace up your sleeve. Whatever's ahead, you can blow through it. Literally."

**Failure consequence:**
"The components are scattered, some lost to the water, some in the hands of teams who think they're holding QRian trinkets. Your precision explosives are gone. Drake without firepower is just... people in balaclavas. Morale takes a serious hit..."

### Jones Junket — Act 1

#### Crew Mission: Retrieve the Ceremonial Whips

In the rush through the hidden passage, the ceremonial whips were left behind. The passage has flooded, and the way back branches into several half-submerged corridors, each with a QRian word carved above its entrance. A longer inscription on the nearby wall is a riddle — decipher it, solve it, and the answer matches one of the corridor labels. One swimmer, one breath, one chance before the water seals them all.

**The dilemma:** The whips are ceremonial, not strictly necessary — but they are deeply tied to the house's identity (their connection to Indiana Jones's legacy). A Jones expedition without whips is an embarrassment; to some, a spiritual failure. Trading for Strange Carved Stones to decipher the glyphs means spending time and scans that could go toward primary missions.

**Consequence if saved:** Whips remain as a house identity item into Act 2. Morale boost — and Jones has proven they can read QRian, which compounds across all future glyph puzzles.

**Consequence if lost:** The house is stripped of its ceremonial symbol. Morale hit; affects later narrative flavor. The team's confidence in their glyph-reading ability takes a blow.

**Puzzle type:** B — Glyph Cipher

**Required clue cards:** 3 cards (distribution TBD). Clue category: "Strange Carved Stone."

**Why other teams have these:** A large QRian translation tablet was smashed during the flood. Fragments scattered across the chamber floor. Each fragment shows a few glyph-to-letter pairings. Other teams grabbed them as interesting carved stones.

**How it works:** The mission card (on phone) shows the corridor entrances with QRian glyph labels rendered in the custom QRian font. A larger glyph inscription is displayed on the wall nearby. Players collect Strange Carved Stone fragments from other teams, each providing 1-2 glyph-to-letter mappings. Using these mappings, they decipher:
1. The corridor labels (e.g., STONE, SILENCE, ANCIENT, OCEAN, TIME)
2. The wall riddle: "IT EATS ALL STONE NONE CAN SEE IT"

The riddle's answer — TIME — matches one of the corridor labels.

**Glyph system note:** This is Jones's first encounter with the QRian glyph system, introducing ~10 glyph-to-letter mappings that remain canonical across all future puzzles. The custom QRian font (designed by Anthony) will render glyphs on the phone screen; players write them on index cards to work with.

**Answer:** `time`

**Accepted alternatives:** `age`

**Hint (after 2 wrong attempts):** "The inscription is a riddle. What wears stone away over centuries?"

**Correct answer reveal:**
"The word settles into the stone like it was always supposed to be there. TIME. Your swimmer drops into the half-submerged corridor and kicks forward into the dark — thirty seconds of nothing but echoing water and held breath from everyone watching the entrance. Then a shout, muffled but unmistakable. The whips are there, bundled in oilskin on a raised shelf, dry as the day they were left. Your swimmer surfaces with the bundle held above their head like a trophy, and for a moment nobody speaks. You just read a dead language off a temple wall and it told you exactly where to go. Dr. Jones spent thirty years trying to crack QRian script. His students just did it in a flooding corridor with fragments and adrenaline."

**Success consequence:**
"The whips are secured and the team is buzzing — not because of what you retrieved, but because of how you retrieved it. You read the wall. You actually read it. The other houses are navigating this temple with torches and guesswork. You're navigating it with the language of the people who built it. Every inscription from here on isn't decoration — it's a signpost, and you're the only ones who can follow it."

**Failure consequence:**
"The glyph riddle is still on the wall and the corridors are still half-submerged, but the water isn't waiting. Somewhere down one of those passages, the ceremonial whips sit on a dry shelf that won't be dry much longer. Dr. Jones trusted you to carry the whips, and the whips are the thing that made this feel like a Jones expedition instead of a university field trip that got out of hand. The deeper chambers will have more words. Harder ones."

### Croft Company — Act 1

#### Crew Mission: Recover the Grappling Rigs

Croft's signature grappling rigs — high-tension cable, Croft-issue hooks, spring-loaded launcher — are stashed on the ledge where the team climbed into the chamber from below. The floodwater is rising toward that ledge. Twenty feet above, a dry alcove sits well clear of the waterline. The team needs to rig a vertical haul route up the chamber wall — a standard Croft operation, if they can find the kit.

**The dilemma:** The route-setting kit spilled across the chamber floor during the initial flood surge. Other teams grabbed the steel components — threaded bolts, clamps, pulleys, clips — as potentially useful tools. Trading for climbing gear while the water rises means spending time away from other missions. And even once saved, carrying heavy grappling rigs through tight passages in Act 2 will slow the team down. But Croft dug in from below, which means the team will need to **climb out through the top** in Act 2 as the temple seals. The gear could be the difference between reaching a high ledge and not — but the weight could also cost precious time in a sealing temple.

**Consequence if saved:** Grappling gear available for Act 2. Specifically tee'd up for a future Act 2 mission: *something glinting in a high corner of an upper chamber, accessible only via grappling hook.* A payoff that rewards the team for making the save.

**Consequence if lost:** Physical ascent in Act 2 becomes much harder. Any climbing-based Act 2 mission is blocked or downgraded.

**Puzzle type:** G — Sequence Ordering

**Required clue cards:** 3 cards across different sets (3+2+2 distribution). Clue category: "Misc. Tools."

**Why other teams have these:** The route-setting kit spilled out of Croft's gear bag during the initial flood surge and scattered across the chamber floor. Other teams picked up the steel components — threaded bolts, clamps, pulleys, clips — as potentially useful tools or interesting metal objects.

| Card | Items |
|------|-------|
| Card 1 | Wall Studs, Rope Clamps & Haul Pulley |
| Card 2 | Anchor Plate & Top Bolts |
| Card 3 | Fixed Line & Cargo Hook |

Each item's description hints at its position in the rigging sequence:

1. **Wall Studs** (item #5) — *"Threaded steel bolts. First into bare rock. Nothing holds without them."*
2. **Anchor Plate** (item #2) — *"Flat bracket. Bolts flush to the starting anchor for a tie-off point."*
3. **Fixed Line** (item #8) — *"Stiff rope. Ties off at the base, hangs the full height."*
4. **Rope Clamps** (item #4) — *"Toothed grips. Bite the rope going up, lock tight coming down."*
5. **Top Bolts** (item #1) — *"Expansion bolts for the alcove. Useless until someone climbs up."*
6. **Haul Pulley** (item #6) — *"Wheeled block. Mounts at the top to run the haul line through."*
7. **Cargo Hook** (item #3) — *"Heavy clip. The last thing on the line. Gear bags attach here."*

**Answer:** `5284163` (Wall Studs → Anchor Plate → Fixed Line → Rope Clamps → Top Bolts → Haul Pulley → Cargo Hook)

**Step-by-step logic:**

| Step | Item # | Name | Forced dependency |
|------|--------|------|-------------------|
| 1 | 5 | Wall Studs | "First into bare rock. Nothing holds without them." Explicitly first — the base anchor drilled into the wall at the starting ledge. |
| 2 | 2 | Anchor Plate | "Bolts flush to the starting anchor." Needs the wall studs as mounting points. "Starting anchor" echoes the studs' description. |
| 3 | 8 | Fixed Line | "Ties off at the base, hangs the full height." Needs the anchor plate as its bottom tie-off point. "The base" refers to the plate. |
| 4 | 4 | Rope Clamps | "Bite the rope going up, lock tight coming down." Needs the fixed line to exist. The mechanism for ascending. |
| 5 | 1 | Top Bolts | "Useless until someone climbs up." Someone must have ascended (via clamps on the fixed line) to reach the alcove and drill these. |
| 6 | 6 | Haul Pulley | "Mounts at the top to run the haul line through." Needs the top bolts as its anchor point at the alcove. |
| 7 | 3 | Cargo Hook | "The last thing on the line." Clips to the end of the haul line running through the pulley. Explicitly last. |

**Correct answer reveal:**
"Stud. Plate. Line. Clamp. Climb. Bolt. Pulley. Hook. Seven steps, no hesitation — your hands know this sequence the way your lungs know breathing. The route goes up clean: twenty feet of ancient stone, the fixed line humming with tension, the haul pulley spinning true. The grappling rigs come up heavy and swinging, and when they settle on the alcove floor, the whole team just stands there for a second, catching their breath. Down below, the water swallows the entry ledge. Another few minutes and the rigs would have been gone. They're heavy. They're going to slow you down. And you wouldn't trade them for anything."

**Success consequence:**
"The grappling rigs ride on your strongest backs, and nobody complains. You set a route up a wall in a flooding temple with scattered kit and no safety net — and it held. Lara would have done it faster. But she'd have done it the same way. The other teams watched you work. They don't have cable. They don't have hooks. Whatever's above you in this temple, you can reach it. That's not confidence — that's equipment."

**Failure consequence:**
"The water took the entry ledge, and everything on it. Cable, hooks, launcher — all of it sitting under dark water that's still rising. You had the kit. You had the training. You just couldn't get it rigged in time. Croft Company without grappling gear is a climbing team with nothing to climb with. When this temple pushes everyone upward — and it will — the other houses will improvise. You'll just... look up."

## Act 1 Flood Missions

One per house. Each house approaches the flood problem through the lens of their identity — Drake forces, Jones analyzes, Croft excavates. The flood cannot be fully stopped (the 20-minute timer is fixed), but successful missions may buy narrative time, protect other missions from damage, or prevent losses.

### Drake — The Stone Wheel

**Puzzle type:** B — Glyph Cipher

**Required clue cards:** 3 cards (2+2+1 mapping distribution). Clue category: "Wheel Fragment."

**Narrative:** All houses blame Drake's dynamite for the flooding. Drake finds a heavy stone wheel embedded in the wall — channels radiate across the floor. Their tactical eye reads it instantly: this controls the water flow. But it won't budge by force. Five QRian glyphs are carved into the wheel's face — the unlock command.

**How it works:** The wheel's face shows 6 groups of glyphs separated by carved dividers. Clue cards ("Wheel Fragments") provide glyph-to-letter mappings. Players decode each group, getting letter chunks that cross word boundaries:

**PUR | GET | HE | F | LO | OR**

The chunks look like small English words (GET, HE, OR) — red herrings. The solve is concatenating all chunks into one string and re-parsing the word boundaries:

**PURGETHEFLOOR** → **PURGE THE FLOOR** → answer: `purge`

The QRians designed the flood as a ritual purge — a designed cleansing, not a catastrophe. "Purge the floor" is the mechanism's activation command.

**Letters (10 unique):** P, U, R, G, E, T, H, F, L, O
- Known from Jones Crew: T, E, L, O (4 known)
- New: P, U, R, G, H, F (6 new mappings)

**Clue card distribution:**

| Card | Mappings |
|------|----------|
| Card 1 | P, U + confirms T, E |
| Card 2 | R, G + confirms L, O |
| Card 3 | H, F |

**Answer:** `purge`

**Accepted alternatives:** `purge the floor`

**What it reveals:** When turned, the wheel redirects water, exposing floor inscriptions. The channels are *original features* — the flood isn't Drake's fault. It's a designed system. The temple was built to purge itself. This exonerates Drake and reveals the temple is an active machine, not a passive ruin.

**The wheel can be destroyed** — once unlocked, Drake can choose to smash it (permanently disabling water control, with Act 2 consequence implications).

**Hint:** "Ignore the dividers between glyph groups. Read all the letters as one continuous string, then find where the real words begin and end. The answer is the command verb."

**Correct answer reveal:**
*"The wheel turns. Water redirects through ancient channels, and floor inscriptions emerge — the channels are original features. The flood isn't from the dynamite. The temple was built to purge itself. Your blast didn't cause this. The QRians did."*

**Success consequence:**
*"Drake is exonerated. The flood is by design — and now everyone knows it. The other teams owe you an apology. They won't give you one."*

**Failure consequence:**
*"The water surge compromised your remaining precision explosives. You'll need to find another way to clear sealed passages in the chambers above."*

**Reinforces house identity:** Drake reads the mechanism instantly but can't muscle through it. The frustration of knowing what it does but needing glyphs to unlock it is peak Drake tension. Decoding PURGE — thinking before acting — is the growth moment.

### Jones — Read the Ancient Drainage

**Puzzle type:** O — Fragment Assembly

**Required clue cards:** 3 cards (3+2+2 tile distribution). Clue category: "Channel Stone."

**Narrative:** Jones recognizes valve controls disguised as decorative inscriptions along the walls. The drainage system is a 3×3 pipe network — 9 sections arranged in a grid. SOURCE (top-left) and DRAIN (bottom-right) are visible, but 7 connecting sections are missing — their carved stone tiles scattered across the chamber.

**How it works:** The mission card shows a 3×3 grid with SOURCE (opening Right) at top-left and DRAIN (opening Left) at bottom-right. Players receive 7 pipe tiles on clue cards, draw each on an index card, and place them in the grid by matching pipe openings on shared edges. **The water must flow through all 9 sections** — the QRians designed the network as a single continuous path, no shortcuts.

**The 9 tiles:**

```
        Col A           Col B           Col C
     ┌─────────────┬─────────────┬─────────────┐
     │   SOURCE    │     V1      │     V2      │
R1   │      ○───R  │  L───────R  │  L───┐      │
     │   FIXED     │  MISSING    │  MISSING    │
     ├─────────────┼─────────────┼──────↓──────┤
     │   V5 ☠      │     V3      │     V4      │
R2   │      ┌───R  │  L───┴───R  │  L───┘      │
     │      │      │  (T=dead)   │             │
     │   MISSING   │  MISSING    │  MISSING    │
     ├──────↓──────┼─────────────┼─────────────┤
     │     V6      │             │    DRAIN    │
R3   │      └───R  │  L───────R  │  L───○      │
     │   MISSING   │  MISSING    │   FIXED     │
     └─────────────┴─────────────┴─────────────┘
```

**Flow path (snake):** SOURCE → V1 → V2 → V4 → V3 → V5(sabotaged) → V6 → DRAIN

**The 7 missing tiles (on clue cards):**

| Tile | Openings | Label | Card |
|------|----------|-------|------|
| Straight | L, R | V1 | Card 1 |
| Elbow | L, B | V2 | Card 1 |
| Elbow | R, B | V5 (SABOTAGED) | Card 1 |
| T-junction | L, R, T | V3 | Card 2 |
| Elbow | T, L | V4 | Card 2 |
| Elbow | T, R | V6 | Card 3 |
| Straight | L, R | (no valve) | Card 3 |

**Why all-9-sections matters:** Only one continuous path visits all 9 grid positions from SOURCE(R) to DRAIN(L) — the snake. This constrains 34 possible arrangements down to 2, both producing the same answer. The T-junction's extra upward pipe is a dead end (misdirection).

**Sabotage:** V5 is marked SABOTAGED on its tile — deliberate chisel marks, same QRian tools. The drainage can't fully function. The QRians disabled their own safety system.

**Answer:** `12436` (valve numbers in flow order, skipping V5)

**Accepted alternatives:** `1 2 4 3 6`, `V1 V2 V4 V3 V6`

**Hint:** "The water must pass through every section. There's only one path that does. Trace it from SOURCE to DRAIN, noting each valve — except the broken one."

**Correct answer reveal:**
*"The valves click open in sequence and water flows through channels that haven't moved in centuries. But V5 stays dead — deliberate chisel marks, QRian tools. They built a drainage system then crippled it. The QRians didn't just build a flood trap. They made sure no one could undo it."*

**Success consequence:**
*"The drainage holds. The inscriptions in the upper chambers survive the flood. You can't fix what the QRians broke — but you saved what matters."*

**Failure consequence:**
*"The upper chamber inscriptions were partially washed away. Key context is missing. Whatever those walls said, the flood took it."*

**Why other teams have tiles:** The carved stone pipe tiles were dislodged from the wall by centuries of water damage and scattered across the chamber. Other teams picked up the decorative-looking carved stones.

**Reinforces house identity:** Scholarly, patient, rewarded by careful analysis. Jones reads infrastructure the way other teams read treasure — three months of schematics trained them for exactly this. The sabotage discovery is the gut punch: Jones solved the puzzle correctly, but the QRians anticipated that someone would.

### Croft — The Stone Jigsaw

**Puzzle type:** O — Fragment Assembly

**Required clue cards:** 3 cards (2+2+1 tile distribution). Clue category: "Seal Stone."

**Narrative:** Water pours through a crack where a sealed passage gave way. Five flat stone fragments that originally sealed it are scattered on the floor. Each fragment has a carved symbol on its left edge, a different symbol on its right edge, and a letter on its face. Adjacent fragments must have matching edge symbols. When correctly assembled in a line, the letters spell the answer. The assembled mosaic also reveals that a "worship scene" is actually an engineering diagram — math disguised as sacred imagery.

**The 5 tiles:**

| Tile | Left edge | Right edge | Letter |
|------|-----------|------------|--------|
| 1 | ☆ | ● | S |
| 2 | ● | ▲ | T |
| 3 | ▲ | ◆ | O |
| 4 | ◆ | ◗ | N |
| 5 | ◗ | ♦ | E |

**Edge-matching forces unique order:** ☆ appears only on tile 1's left (no tile has ☆ as a right edge). ♦ appears only on tile 5's right (no tile has ♦ as a left edge). The chain is forced: 1→2→3→4→5.

**Answer:** `stone`

**Clue card distribution:**

| Card | Tiles |
|------|-------|
| Card 1 | Tiles 1 (S) and 2 (T) |
| Card 2 | Tiles 3 (O) and 4 (N) |
| Card 3 | Tile 5 (E) |

Each card describes the tile's edge symbols and letter. Players draw them on index cards and physically arrange by matching edges.

**Why other teams have them:** The fragments were blasted from the wall by water pressure and scattered across the chamber. Other teams picked up the smooth, carved stones.

**Hint:** "Match the edge symbols — each tile's right symbol must match the next tile's left symbol. Start with the tile whose left edge has no match."

**Correct answer reveal:**
*"The fragments lock together. The crack seals. And across the assembled surface — what looked like figures in prayer are actually construction diagrams. The 'worship scene' is a blueprint. The QRians dressed their engineering in sacred imagery."*

**Success consequence:**
*"The passage holds. Whatever route Croft planned through here stays open. And the blueprint on the stones — that's worth studying later."*

**Failure consequence:**
*"The passage is impassable. You'll have to route around, losing time."*

**Reinforces house identity:** Physical, hands-on, excavation-adjacent. Croft solves with their hands. The stones feel like artifacts — staying Lara-coded even in a defensive mission.

### Success/failure model for flood missions

Flood missions operate on **loss aversion**, not reward. They do NOT mechanically alter the 20-minute Act 1 timer.

**Success:** Nothing bad happens. Act 2 proceeds as it should. The host's act-break narration names exactly what was saved — concrete and legible (e.g., *"Your precision explosives stayed dry. You'll need them."*).

**Failure:** A specific Act 2 mission gets a **warning** consequence attached (via the existing consequence system) — making it harder, not removing it entirely. Warnings sting without amputating content.

Per-house failure consequences (proposed, tune later):

- **Drake fails Controlled Collapse:** Warning on an Act 2 mission — *"The water surge compromised your remaining precision explosives — you'll need to find another way to clear sealed passages."*
- **Jones fails Drainage:** Warning on an Act 2 Temple Investigation mission — *"The inscriptions here were partially washed away — key context is missing."*
- **Croft fails Stone Jigsaw:** Warning on an Act 2 mission — *"The passage you'd planned is impassable; you have to route around, losing time."*

Crew mission failures already work the same way (lost whips, lost fuse charges, lost grappling gear). Act 1 as a whole is thematically "protect what matters before the temple takes it."

## Act 1 Temple Investigation Missions — Framework

Three per house (9 total). These deliver the Act 1 knowledge baseline.

### Mission Brief Formula

Each Temple Investigation mission brief (on the story card) follows this structure, written in **second person**:

**1. Sight of Interest** — Something the team notices in the chamber that demands attention:

| Sight Type | Examples |
|------------|----------|
| **Unreachable artifact** | A glistening vase on a ledge, a large strange-looking astrolabe, a shimmering object half-submerged |
| **Unreachable utility** | A strange visible lever, a sliding construction in the wall, a mechanism with moving parts |
| **Weird path / possibility of escape** | A darkened hallway, a handhold leading to a pockmarked upper area, a gap in the wall with air flowing through |

**2. The Challenge** — What the team must do to reach/access/investigate the sight. This is where the puzzle lives. May require specific clue cards, tools, or collaboration.

**3. Resulting Evidence** — What investigation reveals. Each result is one or more of:

| Evidence Type | Description |
|---------------|-------------|
| **Previous expedition** | What an earlier group uncovered, or what their existence here reveals |
| **QRian suffering** | Evidence of pain, desperation, confinement, loss |
| **QRian technological might** | Impossibly advanced techniques, engineering, materials |
| **QRian culture** | Religion, language, art, daily life, social structure |
| **Self-influence by the Source** | Players themselves realize something is affecting their own conscious experience. **Act 2+ only — not used in Act 1.** |

### Example mission brief (second-person style)

> **Investigate the Impossible Artifact**
>
> Far off to the left, right at the fuzzy edge of the shadows, your team notices, right atop a ledge, a vibrantly red, sparkling porcelain vase. It seems impossibly intact and clean after all these years. But how? What must it mean? You could wander over to the area and find a way to work together to reach the edge of the 15-foot ledge. You'll just need some tools to scale the wall... Upon solving the mission and getting access to the vase, investigation may reveal that not only is the vase perfectly elliptical, it shows the QRians had access to pottery techniques hundreds of years more advanced than their contemporaries... How is that possible?
>
> - **Sight:** Unreachable artifact (porcelain vase on a 15-foot ledge)
> - **Evidence:** QRian technological might

Each house gets one of each sight type (artifact, utility, path).

### Drake Delegation — Temple Investigation

#### Drake T1 — The Shadow Astrolabe *(Unreachable Artifact)*

**Sight:** At the center of the chamber — a stone table, and on it, a thick glass dome. Through the glass: a strange contraption with golden concentric rings, nested inside each other, etched with markings so fine they shimmer. Sealed under glass you cannot safely break without possibly destroying the object. The base has five empty slots, each with a dial of colors — a combination lock.

**Challenge:** Find the five Strange Discs scattered across the chamber (some picked up by other teams) and use them to crack the combination. Drake's tactical eye — the ability to read a system and find the weak points — is the asset.

**Evidence:** QRian technological might + built to trap. The device maps something, but not stars. The geometric patterns correspond to the temple's own internal layout — it's a building map. It shows chambers they haven't found yet. Every corridor, every staircase, every passage was designed to trap people — the halls that seem to lead out curve back in. Labels appear in two distinct glyph styles (early fluid, late geometric), showing the language changed even during the building's use.

**Danger:** This isn't just a building with no exit. It's a trap engineered to be inescapable — one that tricks you into thinking you can get out. And you're holding the blueprint.

**Puzzle type:** N — Pattern Completion

**Required clue cards:** 5 cards across different sets (distribution TBD). Clue category: "Strange Discs."

**How it works:** The dome's base has five slots, each with a dial of colors. Five stone discs are scattered across the chamber — each has a color sequence with one color missing, and a single word etched beneath. Each sequence represents something real that changes color. Players figure out what each sequence represents, determine the missing color, and set the corresponding dial. Enter the five missing colors in disc order.

**The 5 Discs:**

| Disc | Sequence | Clue | Concept | Answer |
|------|----------|------|---------|--------|
| 1 | Black → Indigo → Orange → **[?]** → Orange → Indigo → Black | *Up* | Color of the sky through a day — noon at the center | **Blue** |
| 2 | **[?]** → Yellow → Brown → Black | *Curved* | A banana's lifecycle — what color before it ripened? | **Green** |
| 3 | Red → **[?]** → Brown | *Rare* | Meat doneness — rare to well done | **Pink** |
| 4 | Green → Yellow → Orange → Red → **[?]** | *Harvest* | Autumn leaves through to death | **Brown** |
| 5 | **[?]** → Red → Orange → Yellow | *Forge* | Iron in a forge — what color before it started glowing? | **Silver** |

**Answer:** `blue green pink brown silver`

**Correct answer reveal:**
"Click. Click. Click. Click. Click. The dome splits along hidden seams and opens like a flower. The contraption is heavier than it looks. Up close, the etchings aren't decorative — they're architectural. This device maps the temple itself. Every corridor, every chamber. 7 words, 26 letters, 12 unique letters: T, E, L, M, Y, C, H, I, D, O, V, S.
