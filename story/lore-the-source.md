# The Source — Core Mythology

This is the central secret of the temple. Players learn this gradually across Act 2, with the full picture emerging by the Act 2 revelation.

## The Truth

The geographic location of the temple sits on a force called **the Source**. It is not sentient — it is like a kind of radiation. It imposes a compulsion — a personality of obsession with order — on anyone who passes by or settles nearby for a while.

**Individual effect:** The Source does not affect emotions or physicality. It deeply affects **priorities**. An exposed person might spend hours trying to solve a theorem and simply not care about eating. The drive toward order and pattern overwhelms all other concerns — survival, relationships, basic needs.

### Phase 1 — The Gift

In the beginning, the Source was a godsend:
- Farmers became scholars
- Merchants became eminent mathematicians
- Civilization's progress exploded
- Careful rituals were developed to expose the populace to the Source in controlled doses

The QRian language itself changed, gradually evolving to reflect how they thought — more "cube-like" (this is the in-world origin of the blocky geometric glyph system).

### Phase 2 — The Obsession

The newfound preoccupations slowly became unhealthy or destructive obsessions. It showed in small things first:
- People would only sit at tables with a prime number of people
- Ceramics, barrels — all creations became square-like
- Religion and mathematics/ordered subjects fused together, producing new roles:
  - **Priest Scientists**
  - **Prophet Mathematicians**
  - **Philosophical Clergy**
- *(Players may encounter messages, inscriptions, or logs from named individuals with these titles in clue cards and mission content)*

### Phase 3 — The Contagion

The QRians tried to contain the "logical contagion," but it didn't work:
- People sneaked to the Source area by cover of night
- The mental contagion was impossible to stop
- They came to believe this unstoppable, irresistible force was the direct manifestation of the devil — or the manifestation of a creeping, indestructible evil
- Left unchecked, it could and would destroy everyone — allies and enemies alike

### Phase 4 — The Seal

Using slaves still unexposed to the "Chaotic Order," the QRians:
- Built the temple, making physical access to the Source's epicenter impossible
- Etched warnings and pleadings on every surface — aimed at any future people who might find the location
- Designed it so anyone who entered would not be allowed to exit

The QRians went extinct. Their civilization did not survive in diminished form — it ended completely. The temple and its warnings are all that remain.

## Key Terms

- **The Source** — the geographic force/compulsion at the epicenter
- **Chaotic Order** — the QRians' name for the contagion effect. Order that becomes chaos. Logic that becomes madness.
- **Logical contagion** — the spreading, addictive effect of exposure to the Source

## Design Implications

- The glyph system's blocky, geometric nature is *explained by the lore* — the language literally evolved under the Source's influence
- Act 2's "corruption" mechanic (glyphs shifting on re-scan) is the Source affecting the players' translators
- The temple isn't protecting treasure — it's a prison/containment structure
- The Act 3 decision (seal, collapse, or proceed) carries real weight because the Source is seductive, not just dangerous
