# Puzzle Type Bank

Alphabetized catalog of puzzle mechanics for Temple of the QRians. Each type can be used in multiple missions across acts. New types can be added at any time.

"Glyphs" refers to the QRian writing system — blocky, geometric symbols used as flexible iconography throughout the game.

**Unified glyph system:** The glyphs are consistent across all puzzle types — the same alphabet/symbol set appears whether used as letters (B), concepts (C), math (H), or other modes. This is a deliberate design choice: knowledge compounds across puzzles, and — critically — the glyphs **change as a plot point**. This ties into Act 2's "Corruption" arc where translations become unreliable and symbols shift on re-scan. The glyph mutation is story, not bug.

---

## A — Anagram Assembly

Players receive discrete chunks of letters and must rearrange them to form correct phrases or sentences.

## B — Glyph Cipher

Glyphs represent individual letters. Teams must crack the mapping from geometric symbols to the alphabet.

## C — Conceptual Decipherment

Glyphs represent concepts or words rather than letters. For example, two glyphs meaning "bright" and "head" combine to mean "wisdom." Teams must figure out the conceptual mappings.

## D — Maze Routing

Players are given a maze with multiple possible paths. To solve a puzzle or riddle at the end of the maze, they must route their line along the correct path. Clue cards gathered from scanning provide constraints that eliminate wrong paths — e.g., "avoid all turns at flowers," "avoid all rocks on the maze." The challenge is that many plausible routes exist; only by combining enough clues can players determine the single correct one.

*Implementation note: This is one of the more practically complex puzzle types to produce. The maze itself would be a printed sheet; clue cards deliver the routing constraints.*

## E — Grid Phrasal Reconstruction

A large sheet of paper displays many glyphs in a grid. Other clues provide coordinates that guide players to specific grid positions. The glyphs at those coordinates, when assembled and the rest ignored, form a meaningful phrase.

## F — QRian Connections

Adapted from the NYT "Connections" format. Players group items (glyphs, English words, or a mix) into themed categories. Themed within the world of the QRians.

## G — Sequence Ordering

Players arrange a series of described images into the correct order. Perhaps each clue card provides ~3 images, and 9 total described images must be sequenced correctly. A story may be told through the images when ordered.

## H — Glyph Arithmetic

Glyphs function at a mathematical level. Some glyphs are variables, some are operations. Players must decode the system and solve equations.

## I — Symbol Word Search

A grid contains symbols or glyphs. Players find specific lexographs within the grid. When all found items are connected (their positions traced), they form a recognizable letter or symbol (e.g., the letter K, a cross).

## J — Hidden Symbol Hunt

Glyphs or symbols are concealed within a busy page (possibly embedded in illustrations, patterns, or text). Players must identify and locate them all.

## K — Reverse Decipherment

Language decipherment in reverse — players work from English back into glyphs. Two flavors:

1. **Conceptual decomposition:** Given a word like "Tiger," players must decompose it into its glyph components ("Big" + "Cat") using knowledge gathered from other clue cards. The actual puzzles would be harder than this example — the decompositions less obvious, requiring real synthesis of accumulated glyph knowledge.

2. **Sentence encoding:** Given an English sentence, players must encode it into glyphs. Made harder by the fact that QRian grammar uses a different word order (e.g., Object-Verb-Subject), so direct translation doesn't work — players must also internalize the grammar rules.

## L — Temporal Glyphs

Players encounter glyphs and must realize they describe time. The "aha" moment is recognizing that the symbols aren't spatial or linguistic — they're temporal. The puzzle mechanic is built around that realization and whatever decoding follows from it.
