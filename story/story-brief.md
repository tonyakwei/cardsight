# Temple of the QRians — Story Brief

## Premise

Three expedition teams enter a sealed temple built by a recently discovered civilization called the **QRians**. The QRians had a language built from blocky geometric symbols. The temple was sealed centuries ago with warnings carved into every surface.

## Three Acts, Three Revelations

### Act 1 — The Flood

Teams explore the **lower chambers**. Timer: rising water. The teams race to scan and translate as much as possible before the lower level is submerged.

**Revelation:** The temple is not a monument or tomb. It is a **lock**. Something is sealed inside, and every inscription is a warning not to go deeper.

### Act 2 — The Corruption

Teams are in the **upper chambers**. Timer: translations becoming unreliable, symbols shifting on re-scan.

**Revelation:** The QRian language is not separate from what's sealed inside — it is **connected to it**. Every code scanned pulled the thing closer. The translators aren't breaking. They're being affected.

### Act 3 — The Dying Light

Teams are in the **deepest accessible chamber**. Timer: translator power source dying.

**Revelation:** The full truth about what the QRians sealed and why. The teams must reach a **unanimous decision** — seal it again, collapse the temple, or proceed to the source. If they can't agree before the translators die, they're trapped.

## Core Mechanic

Each house holds clue cards representing **artifacts and tools**. Missions on the story card require clue cards with specific scanned categories. Houses must **trade with other houses** to assemble what they need. The social negotiation around trading is the connective tissue between puzzle-solving and storytelling.

## Narrator Role

The gamemaster (Anthony) serves as narrator. Act closings are performed as a ritual:
- Chime
- Lights dim
- Scripted narration from a fixed spot
- Then natural voice for logistics

## Audience & Format

- **Players:** 15–24 across 3 houses (5–8 per house)
- **Duration:** ~2.5–3 hours
- **Setting:** In-person, snacks and drinks provided
- **Tone:** This is a playtest — players know it may be rough around edges and are there to help shape it

## Key Terminology

- **Glyphs** — The QRian writing system. Blocky, geometric symbols (e.g., a square missing its left side with a dot in the middle). Flexible iconography used across multiple puzzle types at varying levels of abstraction (letters, concepts, math, time).
