# The Three Houses

Three expedition teams have converged on the recently discovered temple. Each has a distinct identity, method, motivation, and aesthetic.

---

## Drake Delegation

**Archetype:** Mercenary relic hunters

**Organization:** Part of an outfit that hunts ancient relics "for the love of knowledge" — but by any means necessary. They have been known to use TNT to blow up relics or temples to get entry. They do all kinds of questionable things to access the priceless knowledge of ancient civilizations.

**Current state:** Down on their luck. Their last string of jobs has resulted in failure and losses. They're getting low on funds, possibly facing destitution. This temple is their biggest ticket yet — a new discovery, a lifeline. They are determined to finally eat some bread (figuratively).

**Method of entry:** Used dynamite to blow open the way underground into the first basement layer.

**Aesthetic / costume:** Tactical gear, gloves, boots, balaclavas.

**Act 3 temptation:** Financial desperation. "Save for Research" means survival and a future for the outfit.

---

## Jones Junket

**Archetype:** Academic disciples of a legend

**Organization:** Inspired followers of the legendary **Indiana Jones**. They've had a long academic career attending Jones's seminars and studying under him from afar. Finally ready to start their maiden journey as expeditioners.

**Current state:** Eager, idealistic, meticulous. Once they succeed here, the great Indiana Jones will likely retire, and they will begin their own hopefully illustrious expedition careers. This is the moment they've prepared years for.

**Method of entry:** Through extraordinarily careful analysis of the temple schematics, they found a secret path into the basement. Took them **three months** of schematics and analysis. A joyful process for them.

**Aesthetic / costume:** Practical-but-ceremonial whips. Pencils in their ears. Academic explorers.

**Act 3 temptation:** Academic glory. "Save for Research" means a monumental discovery that launches their careers and honors Jones's legacy.

---

## Croft Company

**Archetype:** Followers of a charismatic (and slightly unhinged) patron

**Organization:** Friends and hired associates of **Lara Croft**. They are drawn to her but also a little frightened by her extraordinary, almost rabid obsession with ancient relics — an obsession that leads her to dig through tombs with abandon.

**Current state:** When Croft heard of this new temple, she immediately dispatched her team. She is convinced, in an almost rabid fashion, that this will be the next biggest thing. The team is executing her vision — slightly anxious about her but loyal.

**Method of entry:** Dug their way into the basement floor of the temple, in tomb-raider fashion.

**Aesthetic / costume:** Fashionable adventurers — jorts, fantastically styled hair, pecs and abs to match. Physically fit, stylish, bold.

**Act 3 temptation:** Their patron's obsession. Lara would *want* them to save it for research. The team is loyal to her, but the fear factor means some may push back — internal tension.

---

## Design implications

- **Distinct entry methods matter.** Each house literally arrived via a different kind of transgression (explosion, secret passage, excavation). This can color Act 1's Crew and Flood missions — each house experienced the lower chambers differently based on how they got in.
- **Three flavors of temptation.** All three houses are plausibly drawn to "Save for Research" for different reasons:
  - Drake: money/survival
  - Jones: career/glory
  - Croft: their patron demands it
  This makes the Act 3 prisoner's dilemma rich — no house is obviously "the selfish one," each has their own pull.
- **Distinct voices.** Mission flavor text and QRian inscription framings can match each house's voice:
  - Drake — clipped, tactical, pragmatic ("primary objective: recover artifact, minimize exposure")
  - Jones — academic, reverential, footnote-heavy ("notes from Dr. Jones's 2003 seminar suggest...")
  - Croft — bold, fashion-forward, slightly breathless ("Lara would want us to grab this first. Obviously.")
