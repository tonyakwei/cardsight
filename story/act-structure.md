# Act Structure & Knowledge Progression

## Pre-Game / Start of Act 1

What players should know entering the temple:
- This was an ancient civilization, recently discovered
- The temple appears to be a religious temple **(this is a misconception)**
- The people had a notable, strange writing system (glyphs)
- They seemed quite technologically advanced
- Each house has individual histories, temperaments, goals, and reasons for investigating the temple

## Act 1 — The Flood

**Timer:** Rising water. 20 minutes real time = 20 hours narrative time. Timer is purely narrative — after 20 minutes, the Act ends. No mechanical card lockout from the flood.

**Act break:** Host narrates what occurs, collects cards, delivers mission consequences, allows inter-house talk, then transitions to Act 2.

**Open design question:** How do all three houses narratively get from the flooded lower chambers up to the upper chambers for Act 2? The temple is designed so all paths lead down, and the lower level is now underwater. The transition needs a believable mechanism — perhaps the rising water forces them up through a passage they hadn't noticed, or the flood itself triggers an opening in the architecture. TBD.

**Baseline knowledge players should have by end of Act 1:**
- It's NOT a religious temple. It was built for a strange cautionary purpose, and there is danger involved
- The QRian language changed a lot during their civilization's existence
- Math, geometry, religiosity, and literacy were all celebrated and intertwined
- The temple was constructed extremely quickly
- It was constructed so that anyone who entered likely wouldn't be able to come out

*(Players may learn more than this baseline depending on how many missions they complete)*

### Act 1 Missions (5 per house)

| # | Category | Description |
|---|----------|-------------|
| 1 | **Crew** | About the expedition team itself — their history, dynamics, goals |
| 2 | **Flood** | Related to the rising water / survival in the lower chambers |
| 3-5 | **Temple Investigation** | Exploring and deciphering the temple's purpose, inscriptions, architecture |

## Act 2 — The Corruption

**Timer:** The temple's sealing mechanism. 20 minutes real time.

The flood in Act 1 wasn't just a natural hazard — it was the **first stage of the temple's containment protocol**. The QRians designed the temple as an active trap: when the lower chambers flood, it triggers a mechanical system that progressively seals the upper chambers. Stone doors grind shut, corridors narrow, passages close off one by one.

Teams can hear and see it happening around them:
- Grinding stone echoing through the chambers
- Doorways visibly narrower than when they first walked through
- Periodic booms as passages seal permanently ("You hear a deep boom from the east corridor — that passage just sealed.")

In 20 minutes, the upper chambers will be permanently entombed. The teams must navigate upward through the sealing labyrinth to reach the top of the temple before every exit locks.

**Why this works narratively:**
- Players learned in Act 1 that the temple was built to trap people — now they're experiencing that design firsthand
- The timer is visceral and self-evident (they can see/hear it), not abstract
- The host can narrate escalating checkpoints throughout the act
- It sets up Act 3: they barely escape to the top, the levels below seal behind them, no going back

**What players learn during Act 2:**
- There are skeletons of people who lived here or nearby — they were carving geometric symbols into stone before they died
- Whatever was here was intimately involved in both the QRians' advancement AND their destruction
- Being in the temple is exposing the teams to whatever danger exists here, and it's time-bound — there's a limit to how long they can safely remain
- **By end of Act 2:** The full truth about the Source (see `lore-the-source.md`)

### Act 2 Missions (5 per house)

| # | Category | Description |
|---|----------|-------------|
| 1 | **Securing Tools** | Acquiring/preparing resources or finding routes needed to escape upward |
| 2 | **Hazard** | Navigating the sealing temple — finding alternate routes, deciphering mechanisms, clearing blocked passages |
| 3-5 | **Temple Investigation** | Deeper exploration of the QRian story, the Source, and why the civilization fell |

## Act 3 — The Dying Light

**Timer:** Extraction countdown. The teams arranged extraction before entering the temple (scheduled helicopter pickup). It arrives in X minutes. They must be at the top of the temple — the only accessible exit now that everything below has sealed — or they're stranded forever.

**Theme:** Escape and What To Do About the Temple. The teams barely made it to the final chamber at the top. The levels below are sealed. All houses are in the same room but still working on house-exclusive missions (4 per house).

### Act 3 Missions (4 complex missions per house + 1 showtime)

Players are all in the final room of the temple. Before making their showtime decision, they can engage in missions that reveal:
- Thoughts of the QRians
- Possible dangers of each choice
- Letters from the Priest Scientists, Prophet Mathematicians, etc.

These missions inform the final decision but are not required to participate in the showtime.

### Act 3 Showtime — The Final Decision

The showtime ends the game (or time running out does). Each house declares their intent:

**Possible options (not final):**
- **Destroy** the temple
- **Save for Research**
- **Keep Secret**

**Prisoner's Dilemma mechanic (under consideration):**
If 2 houses pick Destroy but 1 picks Save for Research, the dissenting house gets to "save for research." It will be narratively hinted that this could make them rich — but the true consequence would be the enmaddening of that entire house. (This route is not confirmed.)

## Design Notes

- **Intentional failure:** Players are NOT expected to finish all missions each act. At least one mission failure per act is by design. This means mission difficulty and count should be calibrated so completing 3-4 out of 5-6 feels like a strong performance.
- **House-exclusive missions with remixed puzzles:** Missions are exclusive to each house, but the same puzzle types can be reused across houses with different answers and narrative framing. This significantly reduces content creation work (design 1 puzzle type × 3 variations rather than 3 unique puzzles).
- **Mission counts:** Act 1: 5/house, Act 2: 6/house, Act 3: 4/house + showtime. Total: 15 missions per house, 45 missions + 1 showtime across all houses (but many are variations of the same puzzle).
- **Consequences:** Mission failure consequences will be designed using the existing consequence system (warning/lock/redistribute). Details TBD per mission.
- **House identities:** Each house will have distinct temperaments, goals, and reasons for being at the temple. Not yet defined — but likely a spectrum from academic to mercenary to idealistic, which would influence which house is most tempted by "Save for Research" in Act 3. (TBD)
