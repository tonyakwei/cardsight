# Temple of the QRians — Act 2 Mission Reference

**15 missions across 3 houses. ~20-minute act under sealing-temple time pressure (stone doors closing, passages narrowing). Expected completion: TBD.**

**Setting**: The Green Department — ancient botanical/medicinal gardens above the Act 1 antechamber. Reached by Drake's Construction Hoist (Act 1 success), Croft's Grappling Rigs (Act 1 success), or a flood-failsafe.

**Source-evidence types delivered across Act 2**:
- **S0** — The Source itself (geographic radiation field, place-bound, indestructible)
- **S1** — Language drift / The Gift (alphabet was concepts before letters; blocky glyphs = Source's imprint)
- **S2** — The Obsession (Priest Scientists, Prophet Mathematicians — religion + math fused; compulsive classification)
- **S3** — The Contagion ("Chaotic Order" / "logical contagion" — they had a word for it and couldn't stop it)
- **S4** — The Seal (temple built by sedated/eliminated slave-builders; civilization extincted itself voluntarily)
- **S5** — Self-influence (players themselves are starting to feel the Source's pull)

**Trio synthesis** (across all three R-typed missions): the QRian script the team has been decoding all year *is* the Source's transmission medium. To write QRian was to be touched. To read it... is what they have been doing.

**Puzzle type coverage**: 5 trios × 3 houses = 15 slots. Each house uses J/M/R/C/N. Types H and D do not appear in Act 2.

**Type:** J — Hidden Symbol Hunt (emergent composition variant) | **Cards:** 3 ("Apothecary Note") | **Evidence:** S4 — The Seal | **Glyph teaching:** B introduced

**Story card brief:**
> Sweeping across the far wall bedded in vines, you stop. What looked like a garden — dozens of flower and leaf arrangements pinned to the vine surface — is actually a grid. The same few shapes, repeating in patterns. Your medic says it first: it's writing. The whole wall is writing. Scattered across the chamber floor are loose working notes — the pharmacist's own records. You'll want to gather those before the wall means anything…

**Puzzle:** Players receive 1 printed grid (the flower-wall as QRian glyphs). Each card identifies one batch code (A, B, C). For each batch, mark all instances of that glyph on the grid bearing harvest-sequence numbers. Connect them in numerical order — the connecting line traces a QRian super-glyph. Three super-glyphs spell **B-Y-E**.

**Clue cards:**
- **Card 1 — Folded Apothecary Note**: "Batch A — Soporific base compound. Mark all Batch A specimens. Connect in harvest order." [QRian glyph A]
- **Card 2 — Ink-stained Apothecary Note**: "Batch B — Activating agent. Mark all Batch B specimens. Connect in harvest order." [QRian glyph B — **NEW**]
- **Card 3 — Torn Apothecary Note**: "Batch C — Terminal compound. Mark all Batch C specimens. Connect in harvest order." [QRian glyph C]

**Answer:** `bye` (alternatives: `BYE`, `Bye`, `goodbye`, `farewell`)

**Hint:** *The pharmacist categorized three batches — A, B, C — across the wall. Mark each one separately. The pattern of each batch, connected in sequence, is what the pharmacist really wanted you to read.*

**Correct answer reveal:**
"The three shapes resolve: B — Y — E. Not a formula. Not a dosing instruction. A farewell. The pharmacist embedded a goodbye in the arrangement of every plant in this room. Batch A was the sedative. Batch B was the paralytic. Batch C was the terminal compound. Together they didn't just quiet the builders — they ended them. The QRians said goodbye to their own labor force in the language of botany, and left the word hidden in the wall they built."

**Success consequence:**
"The wall said BYE. The pharmacist who catalogued every plant in this room knew what the compound did and knew who would drink it. Logged. The Green Department wasn't a medicine garden. It was an execution chamber dressed up as horticulture. Move."

**Failure consequence:**
"The grid means nothing without the notes to decode it. We're carrying a map we can't read. Whatever the pharmacist was tracking in those batch ledgers stays in the wall. File the grid as unresolved intelligence and press on."
