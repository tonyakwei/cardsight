# Temple of the QRians — Playtest Checklist

Working list for the first live run.

---

## 1. Materials & printing

- [ ] **Story sheets** — 3 houses × 3 acts = 9 sheets. Print from `/admin/games/:id/story-sheets/print/:act`.
- [ ] **Mission QR codes** — embedded in story sheets; verify each scans cleanly on iOS *and* Android.
- [ ] **Consequence cards** — Act 2 and Act 3 consequence cards from `/admin/games/:id/act-break/print`. Pick a theme (Space / Explorer) before printing.
- [ ] **Puzzle handouts** — printed grids for the J-emergent missions:
  - Powder of the Quiet Bed (flower-grid; super-glyphs B/Y/E)
  - Reckoning Floor (Arabic-numeral pebble grid; T/I/M/E)
  - Hanging Garden of Names (canopy map; J/A/W)
- [ ] **Glyph reference card** — one per table, taped down. Saves players from rescanning the rosetta cards every inscription.
- [ ] **54 physical cards** — already printed; confirm none missing.
- [ ] **Card distribution sheet** — print from the admin Simulator after the final reseed, so you know which physical card lives at which table.

## 2. Apartment ambiance

- [ ] Lighting low and warm; lamps not overheads where possible.
- [ ] Music plan per act (Act 1 underwater/urgent → Act 2 botanical/encroaching → Act 3 austere/twilight).
- [ ] Speakers tested at the volume you'll actually use.
- [ ] Optional sound effect for stone-doors-closing at the Act 2 transition.
- [ ] Three tables, one per house, with enough space to spread out 18 cards plus solving-paper.
- [ ] A neutral **Trading Zone** (kitchen counter / coffee table) where house reps meet to trade — supports the social mechanic.
- [ ] Couch / overflow seating for spectators or players who arrive early.

## 3. Host narration

- [ ] **Intro speech** drafted and rehearsed — what is this game, how does scanning work, what are you here for.
- [ ] **Per-act intros** rehearsed — set the scene before unsealing each act's content.
- [ ] **Act-break narration** — read the consequence text generated by the admin (it's written for that).
- [ ] **Mission-completion / failure beats** — light celebration / acknowledgment, not too long, keeps pace.
- [ ] **Showtime countdown** practiced — Act 2 "Press Publish" needs theatrical, not bureaucratic.
- [ ] **Finale adjudication** — review `shared/finale.ts` so you can speak the outcome with conviction.
- [ ] Voice modulation: lower for narration, conversational for guidance, urgent for time-pressure beats.

## 4. Player-facing placards / signage

- [ ] **Flow card** at each table: *Inventory → Trade → Examine → Solve.*
- [ ] **House identity** card or banner so players know which table is which.
- [ ] **Trade Zone** sign (literal "Trade Zone" placard above the negotiation spot).
- [ ] **Bathroom / drinks / coats** signage if guests are unfamiliar with the apartment.
- [ ] Optional: a printed list of **what's NOT in the app** — i.e. solve on paper, write things down, the phone is a portal not a textbook.

## 5. Introductory speech

- [ ] What "Temple of the QRians" is — three rival expedition houses entering a sealed temple, three acts, time-pressured.
- [ ] How a card works — scan, see the gift briefly, write things down, put the phone away.
- [ ] How trading works — between houses, in the Trade Zone, by content not by card color.
- [ ] How time works — a clock is running each act, doors are closing, you won't solve everything.
- [ ] How missions work — each house gets a story sheet listing several missions; teams pick which to attempt.
- [ ] Honoring the players — acknowledge they're the first humans to play this and their feedback shapes the next version.

---

## 6. Player communications (pre-event)

- [ ] Invite + RSVP locked at least 4 days before — headcount drives card distribution and house sizes.
- [ ] "What to bring" note: charged phone, comfortable clothes, willingness to write things down on paper.
- [ ] Arrival window vs. start time — buffer 30+ min for late arrivers and onboarding.
- [ ] Optional dietary check if you're feeding people.

## 7. House assignments

- [ ] Pre-decide vs. randomize at the door.
- [ ] Lanyards / wristbands / colored badges in **red (Drake)**, **amber (Jones)**, **purple (Croft)**.
- [ ] If pre-deciding: balance experience / extroversion / problem-solving styles across houses.
- [ ] House size target: 5–8 players per house works.

## 8. Tech & infrastructure

- [ ] **WiFi stress test** — 15–24 phones scanning concurrently. Have a cellular hotspot ready as backup.
- [ ] Confirm `alltogethernow.land` resolves on local WiFi *and* cellular.
- [ ] **Phone-scanning rehearsal** — walk every QR yourself on at least one iOS + one Android phone the day before. Catch typos in card content while you're at it.
- [ ] **Charger basket** at each table — USB-C + Lightning cables, power strips. Phones drain over a 90+ minute evening.
- [ ] **Host laptop** on the admin dashboard the whole game.
- [ ] **Mobile host console** (`/admin/games/:id/console`) open separately for live triage — lock cards, force-trigger showtimes, end-act.
- [ ] Test the act-transition button beforehand against a throwaway game so you've felt the flow once.

## 9. Per-table solving kit

- [ ] Index cards (for anagram-assembly missions where players rearrange words).
- [ ] Blank paper, pencils, erasers, a sharpener.
- [ ] A flat surface (no tablecloths) so cards lie clean.
- [ ] Optional: small bowl per house for collected/finished cards.

## 10. Card distribution

- [ ] **Final reseed** done the morning of, so the shuffle is fresh.
- [ ] Use Simulator (`/admin/games/:id/simulator`) to auto-distribute physical cards to tables.
- [ ] Print or note your simulator output — *you* need to know which physical card sits where.
- [ ] Lay cards out before guests arrive; don't fumble in front of them.

## 11. Time management

- [ ] **Visible act timer** — TV / projector / kitchen timer the whole room can see.
- [ ] Act 1 = 25 min, Act 2 = ~20 min, Act 3 = adjudication-paced.
- [ ] Soft warnings at T-5 min and T-1 min before each act ends.
- [ ] Buffer between acts for narration, redistribution, and bathroom breaks.

## 12. Contingency plans

- [ ] **Phone dies mid-game** — let players share within their house; don't make it a blocker.
- [ ] **Server hiccup** — be ready to reset/restore via admin; worst case, read clue text aloud from your laptop.
- [ ] **A player has to leave** — a house can absorb one departure without breaking. Plan how you'll handle it.
- [ ] **Card lost / damaged** — admin can re-issue or print a replacement QR.
- [ ] **Player can't scan** — pre-print direct URLs for the four most likely cards as a fallback.

## 13. Comfort & hospitality

- [ ] **Snacks** — finger food, nothing greasy near the cards.
- [ ] **Drinks** — water visible at all times; alcohol optional and *off the puzzle tables.*
- [ ] **Coat / bag storage** area.
- [ ] **Bathroom** signage if needed.
- [ ] Open windows / air circulation — 20+ people in an apartment for 2+ hours.

## 14. Capture & post-event

- [ ] **Consent for photos** asked at the start; take candids throughout.
- [ ] **Group photo** at the end (red / amber / purple grouped).
- [ ] **Feedback session** — 5-minute group debrief, then optional written follow-up.
- [ ] Specific things to ask:
  - What dragged?
  - What landed?
  - What confused?
  - Did the trading mechanic feel alive?
  - Did the time pressure feel real or arbitrary?
  - What would you change about the host's pacing?
- [ ] Take notes that night — memory fades fast.

---

## Day-of ordered timeline

1. **Morning:** final reseed; final scan-test on phones; confirm WiFi.
2. **Afternoon:** print everything; lay out cards by table per Simulator output; tape glyph reference to each table.
3. **1 hour before:** ambiance lighting + music check; charger baskets in place; host laptop and console open.
4. **30 min before:** dressed, calm, queued music low; doors unlocked.
5. **Arrival window:** greet, lanyard each player to their house, point to drinks.
6. **At start time:** intro speech → first scan demo → release Act 1.
7. **Acts run:** narrate transitions, read consequences, hold the clock.
8. **After Act 3 finale:** group photo, debrief, thanks.
