The Belief

A Tuesday can be a chapter.

Most nights are fine. The bar is fine. The movie is fine. You go, you come back, you are roughly the same person who left. The week passes. Months accumulate. And one day you look back and realize you don't remember most of it — not because your memory is bad, but because most of it wasn't memorable.

We build experiences that change that. Evenings designed to stay with you — where the structure pulls you somewhere you wouldn't have gone on your own, and the people in the room become real to you, not just faces. You arrive as yourself. You leave having discovered something.

What We Make

No two nights are the same.

Every event is built from scratch — a different mechanic, a different emotional register, a different kind of invitation. A collaborative mystery where the room pieces together a scientist's scattered files. A Rashomon-style tribunal where the truth is up for debate. A magical school where board games become the excuse to drop your armor.

What stays constant: every night has a structure that does the social work for you. You are never left to just mingle. Something is always in play that gives your presence a purpose — and gives the evening a reason to stay with you.

Previous Experiences

Every chapter felt different.

The Forgotten Files of Ira Remyndu

A full-evening collaborative puzzle built around an original fictional world. Guests received a scientist's scattered case files and pieced together the story as a community. Every detail rewarded curiosity. People played for hours without being told to.

Anatomy of Truth

A Rashomon-style tribunal where multiple witnesses recount the same event differently. Guests take roles — advocates, adjudicators — and the truth is decided collectively. The kind of night where people forget they're at a social event and start arguing about epistemology.

The Pigbumps School of Sorcery and Magic

A magical-school-themed board game night. The name is a wink. The theming liberates people from their adult social armor. You can't be guarded when the room has been dressed for a school of witchcraft.

The Philosophy

Did they come out touched?

Not entertained. Not impressed. Not "that was fun." Touched. Changed in some small but real way. On the walk home, feeling like something actually happened tonight.

That is the product. Everything else — the format, the narrative, the mechanics — is in service of that.

"I know for a fact if I had a choice where I could just know that — oh my god, on Tuesday I get to experience a sci-fi murder mystery with a couple strangers, make some friends, and then come back home and just relax — that would be a dream come true."
